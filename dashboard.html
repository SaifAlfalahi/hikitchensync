Evelyn

Hey everyone! I used to be terrible about letting food go bad in the fridge. But then I started using a simple trick: the "Eat Me First" bin. It's just a designated container (I use a clear plastic bin) where I put anything that's nearing its expiration date or needs to be used up quickly. Leftovers, that half-opened container of yogurt, those sad-looking veggies – they all go in the bin. Now, whenever I open the fridge, I check the bin first. It's made a huge difference in how much food I waste. Anyone else have any fridge organization tips?

Scarlett

Confession: I used to toss out most of my vegetable scraps. But then I discovered how to make vegetable broth from them, and now I'm hooked! Seriously, it's so easy. Just save your carrot peels, onion ends, celery leaves, etc., in a freezer bag. Once it's full, simmer the scraps in water for an hour or so, strain, and voila – you have a flavorful base for soups, stews, and risottos. It's a great way to add nutrients and reduce waste. Plus, it's basically free! Give it a try and let me know what you think.
